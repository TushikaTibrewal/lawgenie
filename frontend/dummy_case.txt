In the case of Smith v. Jones, the plaintiff alleges a breach of contract regarding the late delivery of construction materials. The signed agreement clearly states that delivery must occur by October 1st. The materials arrived on October 15th, causing a delay in the overall project timeline and resulting in financial damages for Smith.

Jones argues that the delay was due to unforeseen weather conditions, which should be considered a force majeure event under the contract. However, evidence provided by meteorological records shows normal weather for that time of year in the jurisdiction. Furthermore, precedent in this jurisdiction suggests that standard weather fluctuations do not qualify as force majeure unless they are exceptional and unforeseeable. 

The statute of limitations for this type of claim is 4 years, and this lawsuit was filed well within that timeframe. Smith has extensive documentation of the damages incurred due to the delay, along with the original signed contract.

We believe there is strong evidence to support the plaintiff's claim for damages resulting from the breach of contract.
